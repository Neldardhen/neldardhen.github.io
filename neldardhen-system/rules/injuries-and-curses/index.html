Curses

Curses are permanent effect of magical origin that affects an individual greatly.
There sometime exists way to lift a curse, but the ritual necessary is specific to the curse and must often be additional tailored to the individual.
There are some curses for which there exist no known way to lift them.

Dhampir Curses

There are two different type of Dhampir curses known as the Feral Dhampir and the Soul Parasite.

Feral Dhampir

Work In Progress

Specific values are subject to change for balancing purpose.

A character affected by the curse of a feral dhampir gains the following traits :

  • Bestial Traits : You cannot wear armour ; you barely stand your clothes, but it's ok, you have though skin and fur (1d6 natural armour). You don't use weapon either, you don't need them. You get the Claw slash and bite unarmed attack.
    • Gives 1d6 of natural armour
    • Change skin type to fur
    • Cannot wear armour
    • Cannot use weapons
  • Feral Dhampir Hunger : You need raw meat... and you need to kill it yourself. At the end of the week, roll a d6 for every week you went by without eating raw meat, if the result is greater than your current focus you loose control and go on a hunt.
    • Treated as an Addiction and counts as a Mind need.
  • Sun Sensitivity : Spend too long in the sun and it will start to burn you. You take 1d4 endurance damage at the end of the day for every 2 hours you spent in the sun.
    • Need for sun is set to 0.
  • Unnatural Regeneration : your body regenerates at an unnatural fast pace. You gain Fast Recovery (Body)

Additionnally, character with the Feral Dhampir curse have access to special Feats when leveling up.

Soul Parasite

Specific values are subject to change for balancing purpose.

A character affected by the curse of a soul parasite gains the following traits :

Work In Progress

  • Treated as an Addiction and counts as a Mind need.

  • Sun Sensitivity : Spend too long in the sun and it will start to burn you. You take 1d4 endurance damage at the end of the day for every 2 hours you spent in the sun.
